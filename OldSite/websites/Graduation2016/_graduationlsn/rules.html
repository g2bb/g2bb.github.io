Rules of Conduct

COMMUNITY OF CHRIST TEMPLE
AUDITORIUM BUILDING USE

The Community of Christ Auditorium is a private building which serves as the World Headquarters of the Community of Christ Temple. It is designed primarily for religious purposes, however it is frequently used by other organizations and there are certain limitations applicable set by the church governance applicable to use the facilities. Please note the following:

  • There shall be no disorderly conduct in or around the building.
  • There shall be no profanity or offensive language used by visitors or performers.
  • There shall be no tobacco used anywhere in the Auditorium, including restrooms.
  • The use of illegal drugs and alcoholic beverages in or around the building is strictly prohibited.
  • The possession of any weapons in or around the building is strictly prohibited.
  • There shall be no food or drink in any part of the building except those areas designated for such use.
  • Paper and refuse shall be placed in containers provided for such purposes.
  • Access to the building is limited to those areas provided for the rental agreement.
  • The Auditorium allows no balloons, confetti, banners, streamers, noisemakers or air horns in the auditorium.
  • Failure to abide by the “NO PARKING” signs in the parking lots will result in the offending vehicles being towed at the owner’s expense. [Designated parking areas are on the Directions & Maps page]
  • Parking is prohibited in the circle drives. The drives are used for emergency vehicles or drop-off and pick-up only.
  • Wheelchairs and/or baby strollers are prohibited in the aisles. They must be stored in either the east or west handicap platform areas.
  • Proper attire for guests is requested. No hats or shorts. The Auditorium is a church.
  • Balcony directly behind the stage is closed.
  • Caps may not be tossed, thrown in to the crowd, on stage, or toward individuals. The faculty speaker leads the tossing of caps on the front lawn of the Auditorium following the recessional.
  • Graduates are not allowed to present anything to the R-7 Board Members as they receive their diplomas.
  • The LSN Class of 2016 would appreciate all guests' cooperation to the following rule so all graduates' names can be heard: Guests who shout or use noisemakers will be escored out. The most important part of the ceremony for all attending is to hear the name of the graduate. The readers of names do not stop for crowd disruptions.

FAILURE TO FOLLOW THESE RULES WILL RESULT IN GRADUATES HAVING DIPLOMAS, TRANSCRIPTS WITHHELD, AND NOT ATTEND PROJECT GRAD PER R-7 BOARD OF EDUCATION POLICY.
